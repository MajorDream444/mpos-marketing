## Core Conversion Framework

### The 5-Second Test
When someone lands on the page, within 5 seconds they must know:
1. What is this?
2. Who is it for?
3. What do I do next?

If they can't answer all three — the page fails.

---

### Page Structure for High-Ticket Offers

```
HERO
- Bold statement of outcome (not the offer name)
- Sub-line that names the ICP
- Single CTA above the fold

TENSION
- Name the problem they're living in now
- Make it feel real, not generic

PROOF
- Results, stories, or specific outcomes
- Concrete, not vague

OFFER DETAILS
- What it is
- What they get
- What changes

FAQ / OBJECTION HANDLER
- Answer the 3 most common hesitations

FINAL CTA
- One action. One link.
```

---

### CTA Rules
- One CTA per page — never two competing actions
- CTA copy should describe the outcome, not the action
  - Bad: "Submit Application"
  - Good: "Claim Your Seat" / "Apply for May"
- Button must have visual contrast — never blend into background
- CTA appears above the fold AND at the bottom

---

### Mobile-First Rules
- All text readable at 16px base
- Buttons minimum 44x44px tap target
- No horizontal scroll
- Hero visible without scrolling on iPhone 14
